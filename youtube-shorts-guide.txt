YOUTUBE SHORTS FOR A BLIND SOUND DESIGNER
==========================================

Your core question: "Can I get away with just audio on screen?"

Short answer: NO on Shorts — but you don't need to see to make great visuals,
and the visuals don't need to be complicated. Here's how this actually works.


WHY AUDIO-ONLY DOESN'T WORK ON SHORTS
--------------------------------------

A huge chunk of Shorts viewers watch with sound OFF or at very low volume,
at least for the first 2 seconds. YouTube's algorithm also penalizes videos
where people swipe away fast. If the screen is a static image for 30 seconds,
people swipe. If there are captions and SOMETHING moving, they stay.

So: you need captions and at least some visual motion. BUT those visuals
can be extremely simple and fully accessible for you to produce.


THE 5 SHORT FORMATS THAT WORK FOR SOUND DESIGNERS
--------------------------------------------------

Pick any of these — they're all accessible to produce without sighted help,
because the visuals are generated FROM the audio or from text, not filmed.

1. WAVEFORM / SPECTROGRAM SHORT
   Show the audio's own waveform bouncing as it plays. Add big text captions.
   - Tools: Reaper renders a waveform image. OBS or CapCut can generate
     animated waveforms automatically from an audio track.
   - Screen content: waveform + caption text + your logo. That's it.
   - Zero visual-editing skill needed. The audio drives the visual.

2. BEFORE / AFTER
   "Here's the raw recording [plays]. Here's the designed version [plays]."
   Split screen with two waveforms, big text "BEFORE" and "AFTER."
   - This is arguably the single most viral sound-design format on Shorts.
     Viewers love the transformation.

3. TEXT-ON-SCREEN TUTORIAL
   30 to 60 seconds, one tip per short. Big bold text appears in sync with
   your voiceover. Example: "Want thicker footsteps? Layer 3 things: impact,
   texture, and a sub." Three title cards, one line each.
   - Visual is just text on a solid color background. Done in CapCut in
     5 minutes. CapCut has full screen-reader support on mobile.

4. "GUESS THE SOUND"
   Play a sound. 3-second countdown. Reveal what it's actually made of.
   ("This is a lion roar. It's actually: a cat meow + a subway train + a
   cardboard tube, all pitched down.")
   - Visual: countdown numbers, then the answer as text. Extremely simple.

5. RECORD-A-SOUND SHORT
   Voiceover: "I recorded my coffee maker and turned it into a spaceship
   engine." Then play the source, then play the designed result. Captions
   throughout.
   - Visual: text cards. Optionally a sighted friend can film you at the
     coffee maker in 30 seconds, but it's not required.


HOW TO MAKE THESE WITHOUT SIGHTED HELP
---------------------------------------

  * CapCut (mobile) — has VoiceOver / TalkBack support. You can add text,
    pick templates, and export. Blind content creators use it regularly.

  * Descript — fully accessible transcription-based video editor. You edit
    the video by editing its transcript text. Perfect for tutorial-style
    shorts with voiceover. Generates captions automatically.

  * Reaper + static image — render your audio as a video with a single
    static image (logo + title text) + burned-in captions. Reaper can do
    this via Video processor, or you can export audio and mux it with
    FFmpeg:

        ffmpeg -loop 1 -i cover.jpg -i audio.mp3 -c:v libx264 -tune stillimage \
               -c:a aac -b:a 192k -pix_fmt yuv420p -shortest out.mp4

    Ask a sighted friend ONCE to help you make 3 reusable title-card
    templates (1080x1920, with your logo and a big text placeholder).
    After that you can reuse them forever.

  * Auto-captions — YouTube generates them automatically after upload.
    ALWAYS review them (have a screen reader read them back) because the
    auto-captions mishear technical terms constantly. You can edit them in
    YouTube Studio. Good captions = bigger audience + accessibility for
    deaf viewers too.


WHAT GOES ON SCREEN — THE MINIMUM VIABLE VISUAL
-----------------------------------------------

Every short needs these three layers. That's it:

  1. BACKGROUND — solid dark color OR your logo OR a waveform animation.
     Static is fine. Moving slightly is better.

  2. CAPTION — large text, high contrast, matching what you're saying.
     Non-negotiable. Drives retention.

  3. BRAND MARK — your logo + handle in a corner, small, always there.
     Free advertising on every repost.


GOLDEN RULES (read before you start)
-------------------------------------

  * HOOK IN THE FIRST SECOND. Don't say "Hi everyone, today we're going to
    talk about..." Start with the sound or the claim. "This gunshot is
    actually a slammed door."
  * UNDER 60 SECONDS, ideally 25–40. Algorithm loves tight.
  * ONE IDEA PER SHORT. Don't teach three things. Teach one.
  * END WITH A QUESTION or a "follow for more." Drives comments + follows.
  * CONSISTENT SCHEDULE beats quality spikes. 1 per week for 2 months is
    better than 5 in one week and then silence.


YOUR FIRST 4 SHORTS (a ready-made plan)
----------------------------------------

Short 1 — "I've been doing sound design for 9 years. Here's my single best
           tip." (format: text tutorial — low risk, introduces you)

Short 2 — "Guess what this sound is." (play something weird, reveal the
           layers in a before/after)

Short 3 — "How to make footsteps sound heavy." (text tutorial, 3 tips)

Short 4 — "I'm blind and I design 3D audio. Here's what that sounds like."
           (binaural demo short — plays to YOUR unique angle. This one
           could pop.)

Ship these 4 over 4 weeks. Measure which gets the most views. Double down
on that format. After 8 weeks you'll know what works for you.


HONEST CALL ON "AUDIO-ONLY"
---------------------------

If the thought of visuals is stopping you from starting at all — upload
audio-only with a static image as YouTube videos (not Shorts). They won't
perform as well but they'll exist, and they count as portfolio pieces.
It is infinitely better to have 10 audio-only uploads than 0 polished Shorts.

Then once you have momentum and maybe a sighted collaborator, convert your
best ones into proper Shorts.

Start ugly. Iterate. Ship.
