GIGASOUND PORTFOLIO — AUDIO TO-DO LIST
=======================================
Goal: go from "hobbyist with YouTube uploads" to "employer-ready sound designer"
in 4–8 weeks of focused effort. Ordered by leverage — top items matter most.


TIER 1 — THE CRITICAL PATH (do these first)
-------------------------------------------

[ ] 1. AUDIO REEL — 60 to 90 seconds. This is the single highest-impact asset.
      - Pick your 4 to 6 strongest moments (not full pieces — just the best seconds of each).
      - Order them by energy: strong hook in the first 5 seconds, peak around the middle,
        memorable tail. No talking. Let the sound speak.
      - Tight crossfades. No dead space. No fade-in on the opening hit.
      - Mix for headphones (binaural moments welcome) but check on laptop speakers too.
      - Export 48kHz / 24-bit WAV master + MP3 320kbps for the web.
      - Save as audio/reel.mp3 in the repo and I'll wire it into the Reel section.

[ ] 2. THREE ORIGINAL PORTFOLIO PIECES — fully produced, each one standalone.
      Pick any three of these formats (variety is good):

      a. BINAURAL SCENE (30–90 sec) — e.g., "walking through a rainy alley,"
         "inside a cathedral during a storm," "busy market at dusk."
         Demonstrates spatial placement, layering, ambience design.

      b. SHORT AUDIO DRAMA (60–180 sec) — one scene. Two characters or one monologue.
         Demonstrates dialogue editing, Foley, room tone, pacing.

      c. GAME SFX PACK DEMO — 6 to 10 sounds in a row with quick silences between.
         (UI click, heavy door, magic cast, footsteps x3, impact, ambient loop.)
         Demonstrates SFX design range — what devs actually need.

      d. TRAILER REDESIGN — pull a 30–60 sec game trailer or film clip,
         strip the audio, rebuild from scratch. Present as "original vs. redesign"
         split. This is THE classic portfolio piece — nobody can argue you didn't do it.

      e. ACCESSIBILITY / AUDIO-FIRST UX DEMO — a 30-sec menu navigation for a
         blind-accessible game. This is YOUR niche. Almost nobody else is making this.

[ ] 3. ONE "HERO" BINAURAL PIECE — 2 to 4 minutes.
      Your showcase. The thing you're most proud of. Heavily produced.
      This becomes the anchor of your portfolio page.

[ ] 4. PROPER AUDIO BIO REPLACEMENT (optional, if you want to redo it)
      The current one is good. If you redo it: 45–75 sec max, lead with one sentence
      that says what you do and who you do it for. End with a call to action
      ("hire me / email me / check the reel").


TIER 2 — CREDIT BUILDERS (do in parallel with Tier 1)
------------------------------------------------------

[ ] 5. JOIN ONE GAME JAM — see game-jam-guide.txt in this repo for step-by-step.
      Target: ONE completed jam = ONE credit + potentially ONE testimonial.

[ ] 6. POST ON /r/INAT AND /r/gameDevClassifieds (Reddit)
      "Blind binaural sound designer — 9 years of practice — free/low-cost audio
      for your indie project in exchange for credit + testimonial."
      Expect 5–10 responses within 48 hours. Pick the 1 or 2 that look real.

[ ] 7. OFFER AUDIO ON ITCH.IO FORUMS
      itch.io has a "Recruitment" board. Same pitch. Indie devs need audio and
      can rarely afford it.

[ ] 8. COLLECT TESTIMONIALS as you go.
      After every collaboration, ask: "Would you write me 2–3 sentences about
      what it was like working with me? I'd love to put it on my site."
      Most people say yes if you ask right after delivery while they're happy.


TIER 3 — AUDIENCE + PROOF OF CRAFT (YouTube Shorts plan)
---------------------------------------------------------

[ ] 9. YOUTUBE SHORTS — "How I made this sound" series.
      Yes, you can do these. See youtube-shorts-guide.txt for the format.
      Goal: 1 short per week for 8 weeks = 8 shorts = a body of work
      that proves craft + teaches + builds an audience.

[ ]10. PLAYLIST YOUR EXISTING YOUTUBE WORK
      Group your existing uploads into playlists: "Skits", "Binaural",
      "Sound Design", "Music". Makes your channel scannable.


TIER 4 — POLISH (do anytime)
----------------------------

[ ]11. Record 30 seconds of room tone / noise floor in your space.
      Useful reference clip; also good to have on file.

[ ]12. Build a small personal SFX library (50–100 sounds you've made).
      Organize by category. When a dev asks "can you do X?", you can often
      say "I already have one."

[ ]13. Write a one-page PDF resume. Include: Tools (Reaper, binaural, etc.),
      Selected work (link to site), Collaborations (as they happen),
      Contact. Save as resume.pdf in repo, I'll link it on the site.


WORKFLOW FOR EACH NEW PORTFOLIO PIECE
--------------------------------------
1. Scope it small. A "great 45-second piece" beats a "planned 3-minute piece
   that never ships." Ship the small thing.
2. Produce in Reaper. Master to -14 LUFS integrated for web playback.
3. Export MP3 320kbps. Name it descriptively: binaural-alley-rain.mp3, not track3.mp3.
4. Drop the file in the /audio folder of the repo.
5. Tell me the filename + a 1–2 sentence description + 2–3 tags, and I'll
   add a new work-item block to the site.
