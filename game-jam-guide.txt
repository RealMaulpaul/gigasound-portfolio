HOW TO JOIN A GAME JAM AS A SOUND DESIGNER
===========================================

A game jam is a short game-making event (usually 48 hours to 2 weeks) where
developers team up to build a game from scratch around a theme. They almost
always need audio people, and audio people are always in shorter supply
than programmers or artists. This is the single fastest way to get credits.


WHICH JAMS TO TARGET (best for audio people)
---------------------------------------------

Tier A — big, beginner-friendly, lots of teams looking for audio:

  * LUDUM DARE              https://ldjam.com
    Runs 3x per year. Huge. Has a "jam" track (72 hours, teams allowed) and
    a "compo" track (48 hours, solo only). You want the JAM track.

  * GLOBAL GAME JAM         https://globalgamejam.org
    Once a year (late Jan / early Feb). Worldwide. Has remote/online sites.

  * GMTK GAME JAM           itch.io, runs once a year (usually August)
    Massive. Hosted by Game Maker's Toolkit (big YouTube channel).
    Lots of traffic = lots of chances to find teammates.

Tier B — smaller, run constantly on itch.io:

  * Browse https://itch.io/jams — there are dozens running at any moment.
    Filter by "upcoming" and pick one that starts in 1–2 weeks (gives you
    time to team up) and runs at least 72 hours (panic-friendly window).

Tier C — audio-specific (worth knowing):

  * GAME AUDIO JAMS — occasionally run on itch.io. Search "audio jam."
    Whole jam is audio-focused. Great for visibility among audio peers.


STEP-BY-STEP: YOUR FIRST JAM
-----------------------------

1. PICK A JAM 1–2 WEEKS OUT.
   Don't try to jump into one starting tomorrow. You want lead time to find
   a team.

2. MAKE AN ITCH.IO ACCOUNT (free, takes 2 minutes).
   itch.io is where most jams live. Sign in, join the jam's page.

3. FIND THE "LOOKING FOR TEAM" CHANNEL.
   Every jam has one:
     - A Discord server (linked from the jam page)
     - A forum thread on the jam page ("Team up" / "Recruitment")
     - A dedicated itch.io community post

4. POST A SHORT PITCH. Template:

      "Hey! I'm Paul — sound designer with 9 years of practice. I do
      binaural audio, SFX, dialogue editing, and music. Primary DAW: Reaper.
      Looking to join a team for [jam name]. Can commit [X hours] over the jam.
      Portfolio: [your site URL]
      Discord: [your handle]
      Quick sample: [link to one strong piece or reel]"

   Post this at least ONCE in every channel that allows it. DM people whose
   project ideas interest you.

5. SCREEN TEAMMATES QUICKLY.
   Reply to people who sound organized and have at least a rough game idea.
   Avoid teams with no programmer or no clear scope — those tend to not ship.
   Good signal: they ask what you need from them (asset list, timing,
   deliverable format). Bad signal: "we'll figure it out."

6. SET EXPECTATIONS UP FRONT.
   Tell them:
     - You're blind, you use Reaper with screen-reader accessibility.
     - How you'll deliver files (WAV 48kHz, named clearly, in Dropbox / Drive).
     - Your timezone + hours you'll be active.
   Most teams won't care about the screen reader — they care that audio
   shows up on time.

7. DURING THE JAM:
     - Ask for a SOUND LIST on day 1. "Give me a list of every sound the
       game needs." This focuses the team and prevents scope creep.
     - Deliver in BATCHES, not at the end. Day 2: UI + core gameplay sounds.
       Day 3: music + polish. This way if you run out of time, the game still
       has audio.
     - Keep a file: credits.txt. Write your name + role ("Sound design and
       music by Paul Seigneur") and make sure the team uses it in the final
       game page.

8. AFTER THE JAM:
     - Get the final game's itch.io page link. That's your credit.
     - Ask each teammate for a 2–3 sentence testimonial. Do it within 48
       hours of submission while they're still happy.
     - Add the jam entry to your portfolio site as a work item, with a
       link to the itch.io page and an embedded audio sample from the game.


SCOPING RULES (protects you from disaster)
-------------------------------------------

  * 48-hour jam → aim for 6–10 SFX + 1 short music loop. No more.
  * 72-hour jam → 10–15 SFX + 1–2 music tracks + basic ambience.
  * Week-long jam → full soundscape, multiple music tracks, polished mix.

  If the team wants more than this, push back. "I can do 8 sounds really
  well or 20 sounds badly. Which do you want?" is a fair and professional
  question.


RED FLAGS IN POTENTIAL TEAMMATES
---------------------------------

  * Vague idea, no game design doc, no plan.
  * More than 4 people on the team (too many cooks in 48 hours).
  * Someone who says "we'll use royalty-free music for now" — they won't
    value your work.
  * No programmer. Without one, nothing ships.


YOUR FIRST POST — FILL IN AND USE
----------------------------------

Subject: Sound designer looking for a team — binaural / SFX / music

Hey all — I'm Paul, a sound designer specializing in binaural audio and
accessibility-focused sound design. 9 years of practice. I work in Reaper
and can deliver SFX, ambience, dialogue edits, and short original music.

I'm looking for a team for [JAM NAME]. I can commit roughly [HOURS] across
the jam and I'm in [TIMEZONE].

Portfolio: https://gigasound-project.github.io  (or your actual URL)
Sample piece: [LINK TO ONE STRONG FILE OR YOUR YOUTUBE]
Discord: [YOUR HANDLE]

Drop a reply or DM if your project needs audio and sounds like a fit!


THAT'S IT. One jam done = one credit + (probably) one testimonial + (maybe)
ongoing collaborators. Do two or three and you have a portfolio section.
